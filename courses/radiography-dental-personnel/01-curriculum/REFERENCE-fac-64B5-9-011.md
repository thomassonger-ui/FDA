# REFERENCE — Florida Administrative Code 64B5-9.011

## Authority

- **Rule:** 64B5-9.011, Florida Administrative Code
- **Statutory authority:** Section 466.017(5), Florida Statutes
- **Regulating body:** Florida Board of Dentistry (under the Department of Health)

## Who the rule covers

- Dental assistants (registered or otherwise) who expose dental radiographs
- Any non-licensed staff operating dental radiographic equipment under a dentist's supervision

Does **not** apply to Florida-licensed dentists or licensed dental hygienists — separate scope.

## What the rule requires

Training must cover: radiation physics and biology, radiation safety and protection (operator and patient), dental X-ray equipment operation and QC, image production (film and digital), infection control specific to radiography, intraoral and extraoral techniques, image evaluation and anatomy, basic pathology recognition, and legal/ethical considerations.

Completion must be verifiable — a certificate of completion is the standard artifact.

## How this course satisfies the rule

Every content module (M1–M12) maps directly to at least one subject area above, plus FDOE Standard 17 benchmarks. The final written exam (Topic 13) plus in-person clinical evaluation day (Topic 14) provide summative evidence of both didactic and practical competency.

Each issued certificate includes:
- Student legal name
- Course title + FAC 64B5-9.011 citation
- Total hours (56 online + 8 clinical = 64)
- Completion date
- Unique certificate ID with QR verification link
- FIDA seal + instructor signature
