Scenario 1 of 3
Adult bitewing · suspected overlap
Multiple choice

Your operator took a premolar bitewing on a 45-year-old patient. The resulting image shows clear contact overlap between the upper second premolar and upper first molar.

What is the most likely cause of the overlap?

A
Insufficient kVp — the beam did not penetrate enough to separate the structures.
B
Horizontal angulation error — the beam was not directed through the contact space.
C
The patient moved during exposure.
D
The receptor was placed too close to the lingual surface.
✓
Correct — horizontal angulation

Overlap on bitewings is almost always a horizontal-angulation problem. Patient movement typically produces blur, not overlap. kVp and receptor placement affect density and coverage, not contact separation.
