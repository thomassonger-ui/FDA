## Welcome to Radiography for Dental Personnel

You're here because you're ready to take on one of the most valuable skills a dental assistant can have — and because Florida requires the credential before you can expose a single X-ray on the job.

Over the next six weeks you will:

- **Build the physics foundation** to understand *what* radiation does and *why* safety rules exist.
- **Learn the techniques** — paralleling, bisecting, bitewings, panoramic — the way the ADA and FDA recommend them.
- **Practice the safety** side every step of the way. Yours and your patient's.
- **Close it out** with an in-person Clinical Evaluation Day where you demonstrate it all.

This course is recognized under **Florida Administrative Code 64B5-9.011**. Your certificate will show your name, the course citation, and a unique verification code.

## How this course works

- **~10 hours a week** for 6 weeks. Quizzes are due Sunday at 11:59 PM.
- **Each module quiz** unlocks the next module when you score 80% or higher. You get 2 attempts.
- **The final written exam** is in Week 6, using Safe Exam Browser. 80% to pass.
- **The Clinical Day** is Week 6 at {{site location + date}}. You'll rotate through 7 stations.
- **Your course access** stays open for 90 days from your cohort start date.

## What we expect from you

Four things carry more weight than anything else in this course:

- **Engage** — ask questions, attend check-ins, use the forum. You learn faster out loud.
- **Stay on track** — the weekly pace is doable if you commit to it. It's hard to catch up if you fall two weeks behind.
- **Prioritize safety** — every skill you learn in this course protects a patient. Treat the content like someone's life depends on it, because eventually it will.
- **Professionalism** — dental offices run on trust. What you do here builds the reputation you'll walk in with on Day 1.
