# REFERENCE — Clinical Rubric (Topic 14)

## Structure

- **Competencies:** 7
- **Scale:** 1–4
- **Passing threshold per competency:** ≥ 3 ("Supervised")
- **Remediation:** any competency scored below 3 triggers a 30-day remediation re-test on that competency only

## Rubric scale

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Independent | Student performs the competency reliably without prompts |
| 3 | Supervised | Student performs the competency correctly with occasional guidance — passing threshold |
| 2 | Assisted | Student requires significant instructor intervention to complete the task |
| 1 | Unable | Student cannot complete the task safely/correctly without takeover |

## The 7 competencies

1. **PPE selection and donning** — correct selection, sequence, and fit
2. **FMX paralleling technique** — must score ≥ 3/4
3. **Bitewing series** — must score ≥ 3/4
4. **Panoramic imaging** — must score ≥ 3/4
5. **Image evaluation** — identification of landmarks and basic quality assessment — must score ≥ 3/4
6. **Infection control during radiography** — must score ≥ 3/4
7. **Radiation safety (operator + patient)** — Pass / Fail gate (effectively must score ≥ 3/4)

## Day logistics

- Full scrubs required
- Photo ID
- Arrive 15 minutes early
- Barrier-sequence checklist provided at check-in
- Attendance recorded via `mod_attendance` at 0800
- Schedule: 0800–1700, 7 stations (one per competency) with rotation
- Clinical Day detail: [`/04-delivery/REFERENCE-clinical-day.md`](../04-delivery/REFERENCE-clinical-day.md)

## Sign-off chain

- Clinical instructor scores each competency on the rubric in Moodle (mod_competency or Assignment with grading rubric)
- Instructor of record signs final rubric
- Dentist supervisor co-signs
- Remediation log opens automatically for any competency scored below 3

## Why this is 40% of the course grade

Radiography is a hands-on credential. A student who can pass didactic quizzes but cannot correctly place PPE, operate equipment, or protect a patient from exposure has not earned the credential. The clinical rubric is the highest-weight single component for this reason.
